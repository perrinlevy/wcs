Have a question?
We're here to answer.

What kind of time commitment does the program require?

As you might expect, that depends on how much effort you choose to put into it. Most sections take anywhere from 45 minutes to 4 hours, depending on your goals. Past data on client performance has suggested that completing one section per week is the optimal pacing.

How will I communicate with my coach?

Typically, your coach will engage you via video chat, email, or messaging as you proceed through the program. Depending on your and your coach's availability, different arrangements are possible. Scheduling is always a factor as well. Most coaches plan engagements weeks in advance so last minute changes can be difficult to accommodate.

Is the program available in other languages besides English?

No, unfortunately at this time WorkCoach is only available in English. Because of client interest, however, we are exploring the possibility of internationalizing in the future.

How is my privacy protected?

Your privacy is one of our primary concerns here at WorkCoach. In building our platform, we've taken extensive steps to rigorously protect your information. Enterprise-grade security features are in place to guarantee your complete anonymity.

How best to work through the program's sections?

Because the sections build on one another, they must be completed sequentially. We recommend setting aside a 1-2 hour time block every weekend so you can consistently work through the material. At that pace, most users take approximately 3-4 months to fully complete the program.

How does the 360-degree survey work?

Our enterprise 360-degree survey tool can be used (optional) to assess your performance before and after the course. Instructions on how to setup the survey are included in the introduction to the program.

How are coaches matched with participants? Can I change coaches?

As of late-2016, we use a proprietary matching algorithm to ensure an optimal coach-to-client fit. And yes, requests to change coaches are very unusual (under 5%) but are typically able to be accommodated.
